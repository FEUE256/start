FÈUE

Förenta Evolutionernas Universitets Elektronik (FÈUE)

FÈUE is an independent research organization dedicated to long-term, system-oriented investigation of computing, electronics, and theoretical physics. The initiative exists to explore ideas that require patience, rigor, and a tolerance for complexity.

The work conducted under FÈUE deliberately spans multiple abstraction layers, from firmware and boot environments to high-level theoretical constructs. This allows research to remain internally coherent rather than fragmented across disciplines.

Mission and Scope

The mission of FÈUE is to design, document, and analyze systems that challenge conventional assumptions about how technology should be structured. This includes questioning default architectures, examining edge cases, and formalizing behaviors that are usually left implicit.

FÈUE is not driven by commercial timelines. Instead, it prioritizes conceptual clarity, correctness, and traceability of decisions.

Research Philosophy

Research at FÈUE follows a systems-first methodology. Each component is evaluated not only in isolation but in terms of its interaction with surrounding layers, interfaces, and failure modes.

Experimental ideas are clearly separated from established mechanisms. Hypotheses are treated as provisional and are revised when evidence, reasoning, or internal consistency demands it.

Core Research Areas

Operating Systems & Boot Architecture

Research includes experimental boot flows, minimal initialization environments, and OS-adjacent service frameworks. Particular focus is placed on determinism, auditability, and explicit state transitions during early system execution.

Theoretical Physics

FÈUE develops speculative yet formally structured theoretical models, including research on white holes and non-standard cosmological assumptions. These models are presented as working theories rather than claims of physical truth.

Electronics & Firmware

Investigation of hardware–software boundaries, firmware behavior, and controlled execution environments. Emphasis is placed on transparency and well-defined interfaces.

Active Projects

Fix System Services (FSS)

A modular framework designed to operate close to the operating system layer. FSS focuses on controlled extensibility, explicit service lifecycles, and predictable behavior.

WHEP

The White Hole Existence Principle explores hypothetical mechanisms for white holes and their possible implications within a consistent theoretical framework.

Cyber::Boot

An experimental boot and initialization environment intended to reduce opacity in early system execution and expose system state transitions explicitly.

Documentation and Transparency

All major projects under FÈUE are accompanied by structured documentation. Design decisions, assumptions, and limitations are explicitly recorded to enable long-term understanding and external review.

This approach ensures that future contributors can reconstruct not only how a system works, but why it was designed in a particular way.

Collaboration and Contributions

FÈUE welcomes collaboration from individuals and groups who share an interest in deep technical work and theoretical exploration. Contributions are evaluated based on clarity, rigor, and alignment with the organization’s research philosophy.

External input is encouraged when it strengthens internal consistency and expands the analytical scope of ongoing projects.
